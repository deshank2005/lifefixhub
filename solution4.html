🚰 Pipe Under Sink Leaking? Fix It Like a Pro!

A leak under your sink can cause a wet mess 💦 and even damage your cabinets. But don’t panic — you can fix it yourself in simple steps! 🧰

⚠️ Before you start: Turn off the water supply valve under the sink to avoid flooding!

🔧 Step-by-Step Fix

  1. Clear the area: Remove any items under your sink 🧽
  2. Place a bucket: Catch any remaining water 💧
  3. Identify the leak: Is it from the drain pipe, the trap (U-shape), or the faucet line? 🔍
  4. Tighten connections: Use pliers to gently tighten loose slip nuts or compression fittings 🔩
  5. Replace washers: If tightening doesn't help, open the joint and replace worn-out rubber washers or gaskets 🧼
  6. Use Teflon tape: Wrap threads of metal joints with plumber's tape to seal leaks 🧻
  7. Check for cracks: If any plastic pipe is cracked, you may need to replace that section 🧱
  8. Reassemble: Carefully tighten everything back together 🪛
  9. Turn water back on: Slowly open the valve and watch for leaks 🚿
  10. Test it: Run water for 2 minutes and dry everything to confirm there’s no leak 🙌

🛍️ What You Might Need:

  • Adjustable wrench or pliers 🔧
  • Bucket or towel 🪣
  • Plumber's tape (Teflon tape) 🧻
  • Rubber washers or gaskets 🧽
  • Replacement pipe or trap (if cracked) 🧱
💡 Tip: Take a photo of the pipe before disassembling it. It’ll help when putting it back together! 📸

Still leaking? 🧯 It might be time to call a professional plumber. But 80% of leaks can be fixed at home using these steps! 🔧💪
