🚿 Shower Drain Clogged? Here’s the Complete Fix Guide!

Water pooling in your shower? 🛁 Standing water is a clear sign your drain is clogged. But don't worry – you don't need a plumber for most clogs! Let's fix it step-by-step and prevent future blockages too. 💪

🔍 Common Causes: Hair, soap scum, shampoo buildup, or even small objects dropped in the drain.

🔧 Step 1: Remove Visible Debris

  • Take off the drain cover (you may need a screwdriver).
  • Use a glove or tissue to pull out hair, soap chunks, or other gunk you can see 👀.
  • Dispose of it properly in the trash 🗑️ – don’t push it further down!

🧼 Step 2: Pour Boiling Water

This old-school trick works great on grease, soap, and scum!

  • Boil about 1-2 liters of water in a kettle or pot.
  • Slowly pour it into the drain and wait a few minutes. ☕

🥼 Step 3: Use Baking Soda + Vinegar Reaction

A natural, chemical-free way to break clogs!

  1. Pour 1/2 cup of baking soda into the drain.
  2. Follow it with 1 cup of vinegar. It will fizz up like a volcano 🌋
  3. Cover the drain with a plate or rag. Wait 15-30 minutes.
  4. Flush again with boiling water 🔥.

🪠 Step 4: Plunge the Drain

  • Use a small plunger (flat bottom works best).
  • Add a little water so the rubber part is fully covered.
  • Plunge forcefully 10-15 times. 💥
  • Listen for a gurgle sound — it means the clog is breaking.

🐍 Step 5: Use a Drain Snake (Optional)

For deep or stubborn clogs, use a manual or electric snake:

  • Insert snake into the drain and rotate handle slowly.
  • Pull it out gently – it may bring up a big nasty clog 😷
  • Repeat until water drains freely.
💡 Tip: Don’t use chemical drain cleaners too often – they can damage your pipes over time.

🛡️ Step 6: Prevent Future Clogs

  • Use a **drain hair catcher** or mesh screen 🕸️
  • Clean the cover weekly with a toothbrush + soap 🧼
  • Once a month, repeat the **boiling water trick**
  • Trim long hair over a towel – not in the shower 💇‍♀️

🎉 Done! Enjoy Smooth Showers

If all steps were done, your drain should be flowing beautifully again 💧. If not, the clog might be in the pipes below the floor — at that point, call a professional plumber.
